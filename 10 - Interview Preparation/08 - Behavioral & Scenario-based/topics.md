# Behavioral & Scenario-based

## STAR Method Framework
- **S**ituation — Set the context
- **T**ask — What needed to be done
- **A**ction — What you specifically did
- **R**esult — Measurable outcome

## Common Behavioral Questions

### Conflict & Influence
- Tell me about a time you disagreed with a product manager on a technical decision.
- Describe a situation where you had to influence a team to adopt a new tool or process.
- How did you handle a stakeholder who wanted unrealistic AI capabilities delivered immediately?

### Ambiguity & Problem-Solving
- Walk me through how you approached a problem when requirements were unclear.
- Describe a time you had to make a decision with incomplete data.
- Tell me about a project where the scope kept changing — how did you handle it?

### Failure & Learning
- Describe a model that performed well in testing but failed in production. What happened?
- Tell me about a technical decision you made that turned out to be wrong.
- What's a project you would do differently if you could go back?

### Leadership & Ownership
- Describe a time you mentored a junior engineer through a difficult problem.
- Tell me about a cross-functional initiative you led across multiple teams.
- How have you contributed to improving engineering culture or standards?

## Scenario-Based (Whiteboard)

### System Design Scenarios
- Design an AI-powered customer service system that handles 1M conversations/day.
- Your team's model latency increased by 5x after a recent data pipeline change. Diagnose.
- A key vendor (e.g., OpenAI) changes their pricing model overnight. What do you do?

### Trade-off Scenarios
- Higher accuracy vs. lower latency — how do you decide?
- Build a custom RAG pipeline vs. buy a SaaS solution — walk through the analysis.
- A model has 95% accuracy overall but 70% on a minority subgroup. What's your plan?

### Crisis Scenarios
- A production model starts generating harmful outputs. Walk through your response.
- Your training cluster goes down 2 weeks before a major deliverable. What now?
- A data breach exposes customer data used in model training. How do you respond?

## Preparation Tips
- Prepare 5-7 robust STAR stories covering different dimensions (technical depth, leadership, failure, conflict, mentorship)
- Practice articulating trade-offs out loud
- Have a structured 4-step framework for system design: clarify → high-level → deep dive → scale
