# Governance, Ethics & Compliance

## Responsible AI
- Fairness: demographic parity, equal opportunity, disparate impact
- Bias detection in data, models, and outputs
- Explainability: SHAP, LIME, Integrated Gradients, attention analysis
- Transparency: model cards, data sheets, system cards

## Regulatory Landscape
- GDPR — right to explanation, data minimization, deletion
- CCPA, EU AI Act, China's AI regulations
- Industry-specific: HIPAA (healthcare), SOX (finance), FINRA
- Emerging regulation around foundation models and copyright

## Security
- Model security: adversarial attacks, prompt injection, jailbreaking
- Data poisoning, model extraction, inversion attacks
- Guardrails: input filtering, output moderation, rate limiting
- Red-teaming, automated adversarial testing

## Data Privacy
- PII detection and redaction
- Differential privacy (DP-SGD)
- Synthetic data generation for privacy preservation
- Federated learning — when it applies and its limitations

## Compliance Programs
- AI risk assessment frameworks (NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001)
- Internal AI ethics boards, review processes
- Documentation and audit trail requirements
- Vendor AI risk management (third-party models)

## Common Interview Questions
- How would you detect and mitigate bias in a hiring model?
- What is the EU AI Act and how does it affect AI architecture decisions?
- Design a red-teaming strategy for a new LLM-based product.
- How would you handle a user's GDPR right to deletion request for an ML system?
- What guardrails would you put in place for a customer-facing GenAI chatbot?
