# Business & Leadership

## AI Strategy
- Defining an AI vision and roadmap aligned with business goals
- Opportunity identification — where AI drives vs. doesn't drive value
- Build vs. buy vs. partner decisions
- TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) analysis for AI systems

## Stakeholder Management
- Communicating technical constraints to non-technical executives
- Managing expectations: what AI can and cannot do
- Running AI discovery workshops
- Navigating organizational change — MLOps adoption, data culture

## Team Leadership
- Structuring AI teams: centralized vs. embedded vs. hybrid
- Hiring and mentoring ML engineers and MLEs
- Establishing engineering standards, code reviews, model reviews
- Fostering a culture of experimentation and responsible innovation

## Program & Portfolio Management
- Prioritizing AI initiatives (ROI, feasibility, strategic alignment)
- Agile vs. phased delivery for AI projects
- Managing model lifecycle across multiple products
- Vendor evaluation — model providers, platforms, consultancies

## Measuring Impact
- Defining KPIs: business metrics (revenue, cost savings) vs. ML metrics
- Experimentation: A/B testing for model-driven features
- Reporting AI outcomes to leadership and the board

## Common Interview Questions
- Describe a time you convinced a skeptical executive to invest in an AI initiative.
- How would you prioritize between three competing AI project proposals?
- What metrics would you use to measure the success of an AI platform team?
- Walk through your decision process for a build-vs-buy of an LLM serving stack.
- How do you handle a situation where a model achieves great accuracy but fails in production?
