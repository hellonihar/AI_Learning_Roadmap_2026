# AI Strategy, Roadmap & ROI

Align AI initiatives with business objectives, build compelling business cases, and lead organizational change.

## Learning Objectives
- Develop an AI strategy aligned with business goals and maturity level
- Build ROI models to justify AI investments and prioritize opportunities
- Design AI team structures and operating models for scale

## Deep Topics

### 1. Building a Business Case for AI Projects
- **Problem identification**: pain point analysis, opportunity sizing, feasibility assessment
- **Business value drivers**: revenue increase, cost reduction, risk mitigation, customer experience
- **Quantifying value**: projected efficiency gains, revenue uplift, cost savings with confidence intervals
- **Investment required**: compute, data engineering, MLOps platform, headcount, change management
- **Executive presentation**: narrative structure, data visualization, risk acknowledgment

### 2. Build vs. Buy vs. Partner Analysis
- **Build**: competitive advantage, proprietary data, full control (cost: high, time: long, risk: high)
- **Buy**: commoditized capabilities, speed to market, vendor support (cost: medium, time: short, risk: low)
- **Partner**: specialized expertise, shared risk, co-innovation (cost: variable, time: medium, risk: medium)
- **Decision framework**: strategic value, uniqueness of data, in-house capability, time-to-market requirement
- **Case studies**: build for core differentiator, buy for commodity, partner for niche expertise

### 3. Vendor Evaluation & Selection
- **Evaluation criteria**: accuracy, latency, throughput, cost, security, compliance, data residency
- **Proof of concept (PoC)**: structured evaluation with representative data and metrics
- **Vendor comparison matrix**: weighted scoring across criteria
- **Negotiation**: volume discounts, data processing agreements (DPAs), SLAs, exit clauses
- **Vendor risk assessment**: financial stability, roadmap alignment, support quality

### 4. Defining AI Maturity Models
- **Levels**: Ad-hoc → Repeatable → Defined → Managed → Optimizing
- **Assessment dimensions**: data infrastructure, talent, processes, technology, governance, culture
- **Maturity assessment**: survey, interviews, artifact review
- **Transition roadmap**: specific initiatives to advance each maturity dimension
- **Benchmarking**: industry comparisons, aspirational targets

### 5. Setting OKRs & KPIs for AI Initiatives
- **Leading indicators**: data coverage, feature freshness, experiment velocity, model deployment frequency
- **Lagging indicators**: model accuracy, business impact (revenue, cost, satisfaction), user adoption
- **OKR examples**: "Deploy 3 production models with measurable business impact this quarter"
- **KPI dashboards**: real-time visibility into model performance and business metrics
- **Avoiding vanity metrics**: focusing on proxy metrics vs. actual business outcomes

### 6. Cost-Benefit Analysis & ROI Calculation
- **ROI formula**: (benefit - cost) / cost over defined time horizon (1, 3, 5 years)
- **NPV and IRR**: discounting future cash flows for investment comparison
- **Sensitivity analysis**: best case, expected, worst case scenarios
- **Monte Carlo simulation**: probabilistic ROI estimation with key uncertainty drivers
- **Post-deployment measurement**: tracking actual vs. projected benefits, course correction

### 7. Organizational Structure
- **Centralized AI CoE**: center of excellence serving business units (consistency, reuse)
- **Decentralized embedded**: AI teams within each business unit (speed, domain alignment)
- **Federated model**: central platform team + embedded applied teams (best of both)
- **Platform vs. applied teams**: platform builds infrastructure and tooling; applied teams solve business problems
- **Roles**: ML engineer, data scientist, ML platform engineer, AI architect, ML product manager

### 8. Change Management & Stakeholder Communication
- **Stakeholder mapping**: sponsors, champions, skeptics, detractors
- **Communication plan**: regular updates, demos, transparent reporting of successes and failures
- **Training**: upskilling existing talent, AI literacy programs for non-technical stakeholders
- **Cultural change**: celebrating experimentation, accepting failure as learning, data-driven decision culture

## Deliverables
- Build an AI strategy deck with roadmap, ROI model, and organizational design for a mid-market company
- Perform a build-vs-buy analysis for a specific AI capability with a weighted scorecard
- Create a maturity assessment and 12-month improvement plan for an AI organization
